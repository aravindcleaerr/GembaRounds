How Gemba Walks Improve Manufacturing

From Toyota's shop floor to your factory — the practice that transforms operations from reactive to proactive.

By PS Satish Sir & Team | April 2026 | 8 min read

The Problem: Invisible Factory Floor

In most manufacturing companies today, managers spend 80% of their time in offices — reading reports, attending meetings, and making decisions based on second-hand information. The shop floor, where value is actually created, remains largely invisible.

What is a Gemba Walk?

Gemba (現場) is Japanese for "the actual place." In manufacturing, it means the shop floor — where value is created. A Gemba Walk is the practice of leaders going to the floor to observe, connect, and improve.

"Go see, ask why, show respect." — Taiichi Ohno, Toyota Production System

It is NOT an audit. It is NOT an inspection. It is about understanding reality, connecting with people, and building a culture of continuous improvement.

Key Insight

The biggest impact isn't in the observations themselves — it's in the culture change. When workers see managers on the floor regularly, appreciating good work and following up on issues, trust builds and engagement soars.

Getting Started: 5 Steps

  1. Pick one area — Start with a single production line or bay
  2. Walk daily, 15 minutes — Consistency beats duration
  3. Follow COACH — Connect first, observe second, always appreciate
  4. Record digitally — Use GembaRounds to capture, track, and follow up
  5. Review weekly — Share findings with the team, recognize contributors
Common Mistake

Don't turn Gemba walks into audits. The moment workers feel "inspected" rather than "supported," they stop sharing real problems. Start with appreciation (the 'A' in COACH).
